Biography

Hi! I'm a PhD Candidate in the MSCA HYBRIDS project at Universidade de Évora, where I focus on exploring the intersection of Natural Language Processing and social sciences to better understand disinformation phenomena. (Yes, I spend my days teaching machines to spot fake news!)

I hold degrees in Communication Sciences and Sociology & Social Research (cum laude), which provide the interdisciplinary foundation for my current research work. My technical skills include Python programming, and I'm continuously learning in areas such as NLP and machine learning applications in social research. When I'm not coding, you'll likely find me trying to explain to friends why conspiracy theories are fascinating from a research perspective (without endorsing them, of course!).

I am fluent in Italian (native), English (C1), Spanish (C1), and French (B2), and I'm currently learning Portuguese to support my research in Iberian contexts.

Always happy to connect with fellow researchers and anyone interested in the intersection of technology and society!
